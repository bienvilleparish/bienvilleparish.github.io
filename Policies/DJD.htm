EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT

 

 

Employees who regularly make use of their own cars traveling within the Parish on school business shall be reimbursed at a mileage rate set by the Bienville Parish School Board.

 

For travel reimbursement in their own vehicles, employees shall be required to carry a minimum insurance policy of $50,000/100,000/25,000 covering liability and property damage.

 

The Bienville Parish School Board recognizes its responsibility to provide the opportunity for the continual professional growth of its staff.  It also recognizes the importance of being a well-informed Board.  It is the policy of the Board that Board members and professional staff be allowed to attend state and national association seminars, workshops, and conventions.  However, such expenditures must be within budgetary limitations.  It is with these facts in mind that the following guidelines for limiting and reducing expenditures for out-of-state travel are recommended.

 

  1. Employees who desire to attend out-of-state seminars, conventions, etc., must obtain prior approval from the Superintendent and the Bienville Parish School Board.

  2. Board members who desire to attend out-of-state seminars, conventions, etc., must obtain prior approval from the Bienville Parish School Board.

  3. Approval to attend out-of-state seminars, conventions, etc., should be considered on the appropriateness to the person's job or position and its possible value to the school system.

  4. Approval to attend out-of-state seminars and/or conventions without expense to the Board may be granted by the Superintendent.

 

REIMBURSEMENT FOR TRANSPORTATION, LODGING, MEALS, AND OTHER EXPENSES

 

  1. Transportation

 

    1. A mileage allowance shall be authorized for travelers approved to use personally-owned vehicles while in the conduct of official business for Bienville Parish School Board.  There will be no reimbursement for mileage incurred commuting to/from one's residence to his/her official work domicile.

    2. Mileage shall be reimbursable on the basis of state mileage rates.

    3. Air travel shall be reimbursed only at coach or economy class rates.

    4. Personal automobile expense will be limited to the lesser amount of first class air fare or state mileage rates.

 

  1. Lodging and Meals

 

    1. Meals only (including tips):  Employees, while traveling, may be allowed up to state amounts for meals:

    2. Employees may be reimbursed for meals according to the following schedule:

      Breakfast: When travel begins at/or before 6:00 a.m. on the first day of travel, or extends beyond 9:00 a.m. on the last day of travel, and for any intervening days.
       
      Lunch: When travel begins at/or before 10:00 a.m. on the first day of travel, or extends beyond 2:00 p.m. on the last day of travel, and for any intervening days.
       
      Dinner: When travel begins at/or before 4:00 p.m. on the first day of travel, or extends beyond 8:00 p.m. on the last day of travel, and for any intervening days.
       

 

    1. Lodging only

      Employees shall be reimbursed actual expenses for lodging.  Receipts from a bona fide hotel or motel for lodging shall be submitted and attached to the travel voucher.

 

  1. Meals in New Orleans and Out-of-State

 

    1. Meals only (including tips) may be reimbursed up to state amounts.

 

  1. Receipts are not required for the following:

 

    1. Taxicab or local public transportation

    2. Routine meals (number of meals must be shown on travel voucher)

    3. Telephone and telegraph

    4. Tips for baggage handling

    5. Parking at self-service lots
