STAFF RIGHTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

 

 

  1. Hours of Duty:  With respect to specified hours, teachers are expected to be at school before school opens each day, and sign the register in person in the principal's office.  They must be in their respective classroom and available to students not later than ten (10) minutes prior to the opening of school.  (This does not apply to teachers on duty.)  No employee shall leave the grounds without permission of the principal.  No teacher will leave school earlier than ten (10) minutes after school is dismissed and not earlier than 3:20 p.m.

  2. Outside Activities:  All employees of the School Board are expected to give their full time to school duties insofar as such duties require full time.  The School Board looks with disfavor on engaging or indulging in remunerative or social activities which would interfere with the school work for which he or she was employed, or any business activity which might impair the relationship with other employees or with the public.

  3. Private Instruction:  No employee should engage in private instruction of his or her student for personal compensation during the regular school hours.

  4. Attendance at Meetings:  Employees shall attend all regular and special meetings called by Superintendent, supervisor, or the principal, unless previously excused by them.

  5. Ethical Conduct:  Employees should study the Code of Ethics and practice ethical conduct at all times.

  6. Sending Students on Errands:  Employee shall not send students on errands without permission of the principal.

  7. Phone Calls Discouraged:  Employees are not to make or receive personal telephone calls during the regular school days unless it is strictly an emergency.

  8. Illness of Students:  Employees shall report promptly to the principal any illness of students that may warrant his or her parents or guardian being notified.

  9. Accident of Students:  Employees shall report promptly to the principal all accidents and the employee shall immediately complete the accident report required by the Louisiana Department of Education.

  10. Record Keeping:  The employee should prepare all reports called for by the principal, the Bienville Parish School Board, or the Louisiana Department of Education. These reports should be carefully checked to assure they are correct and should be handed in on time.

  11. Health Program:  Teachers should observe the general health of each student.  If skin rash, sores, or other abnormal conditions exist, the child might need to be sent home.  The teacher should always be on the lookout for signs of communicable disease and should report to the principal immediately any unusual conditions found.  This is for the protection of all students.

 

Bienville Parish School Board
