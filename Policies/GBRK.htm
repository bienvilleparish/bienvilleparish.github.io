ANNUAL LEAVE

 

 

All twelve-month employees shall earn annual leave at the rate specified below.  Annual leave cannot be used until earned.

 

First three years (0-3)

 12 days/year (1 day/month)

Next seven years (4-10)

 15 days/year (1¼ days/month)

Beginning with eleventh year (11+)

 18 days/year (1½ days/month)

 

Beginning on July 1, 2005, all eligible employees must use the earned annual leave during the twelve (12) month period in which it was earned.  In the event that the employee does not use the earned annual leave during this twelve (12) month period, he/she shall be compensated at his/her daily rate of pay for the unused earned annual leave days in excess of twenty-five (25).  The Bienville Parish School Board may not reduce annual leave which has been accumulated by an eligible employee.

 

The Bienville Parish School Board shall pay an employee for any unused annual leave upon termination of employment.  In case of death prior to termination, the employee's heirs shall be paid for any unused annual leave.

 

Annual leave may be used in lieu of sick leave, if requested.

 

An employee shall be required to apply for annual leave in advance of the time annual leave is desired.  The employee's immediate supervisor and the Superintendent must approve the request for annual leave.

 

Employees exceeding their annual leave shall be penalized by salary reduction at their daily rate based on their annual salary.

 

This policy shall apply to all twelve (12) month employees hired on and after July 1, 2005.

 

Employees hired prior to July 1, 2005 may elect to remain under current annual leave policy (File GBRK or GCRH) or they may elect to move to the policy covering twelve (12) month employees hired on or after July 1, 2005. (This decision shall be irrevocable.)

 

Any eligible employee may request payment for accumulative annual leave in excess of twenty-five (25) days.  However, an employee may make no more than one (1) request per calendar year for payment of those accumulated annual leave days in excess of twenty-five (25).
