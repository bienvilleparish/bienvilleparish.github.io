STUDENT ANTI-HARASSMENT POLICY

 

 

The Bienville Parish School Board disapproves of and does not tolerate harassment by employees against/to students, by students against/to employees or by one student against/to another student.  No employee or student, either male or female, shall be subject to unsolicited or unwelcome harassment due to their age, race, sex, religion or other protective classification, including any unsolicited or unwelcome sexual overtures or conduct, either verbal or physical.  Sexual harassment does not refer to occasional compliments of a socially acceptable nature.  It refers to behavior that is not welcome, that is personally offensive, and therefore interferes with the purposes of the employee in the academic, extracurricular or co-curricular atmosphere.  Sexual harassment includes any type of sexually coercive or oppressive conduct, including, but not limited to, threats, comments, jokes, requests, suggestions or overtures of a sexual nature.  Sexual harassment may also include trade-off claims which occur when an employee makes sexual advances toward a student which threaten or imply retaliation if the student resists or rewards if the student acquiesces.

 

COVERAGE

 

This policy applies to all employees, school board members and to all students of the Bienville Parish School system.  It applies at school, school-sponsored events, and in situations which are related to the school.  The term employee as used herein includes all employees of the Bienville Parish School Board and school board members.

 

RESPONSIBILITY

 

Harassment, whether committed by an employee or student, is specifically prohibited as unlawful and against the policy of the Bienville Parish School Board.

 

COMPLAINT PROCEDURE

 

Complaints of harassment which take place at school or at a school related function or arising out of the school setting should be made to the principal of the school.  Should the person engaging in the harassment be the principal of the school, the complaint should be brought directly to the Superintendent of Schools.

 

After notification of the complaint, a confidential investigation will be initiated immediately to gather all facts about the complaint.  Following such investigation, a determination will be made regarding the resolution of the claim.  If warranted, disciplinary action will be taken up to and including involuntary termination of the employee and/or expulsion of a student.  Any disciplinary action regarding an employee will be placed in the employee's personnel file which will reflect the action taken and the grounds thereof.  Any disciplinary action taken in regard to a student will be maintained as any other student disciplinary violation.

 

NONRETALIATION

 

Retaliation against any employee or student who brings a harassment complaint or who assists in investigating such a complaint is expressly prohibited and will not be tolerated.  Any employee or student bringing a harassment complaint or who assists in the investigation of such a complaint will not be adversely affected, discriminated against or punished because of the complaint.
